# Product

## Register

product — an app UI (management cockpit), not a marketing surface. Design serves the workflow, but with emotional warmth: this is the couple's wedding, not a corporate ERP.

## Users

The couple (two people, shared use). Primary user: Gabriel. Context: planning their own wedding over many months — usually on the phone (sofa, commute, between meetings with vendors), occasionally on desktop for bigger reviews. State of mind alternates between excitement and money anxiety. The job to be done: "know exactly where we stand — what's hired, what's paid, what's due soon, what's still missing — without opening a spreadsheet."

## Product Purpose

A private wedding-planning cockpit: vendors, budget goal, contracted values, down payments + installments, due dates, month-by-month checklist and countdown to the wedding day. Success = the couple trusts the numbers at a glance and never misses a payment or a deadline.

## Brand Personality

Elegante, acolhedor, preciso. The interface should feel like a premium wedding planner's leather notebook: warm, calm and celebratory — while the numbers feel bank-grade trustworthy. Emotional goals: calm confidence ("está tudo sob controle") and quiet joy (countdown, progress, conquered milestones).

## Anti-references

- Cold spreadsheet aesthetics (dense grids, gray-on-white, Excel vibes).
- Generic SaaS admin dashboards (navy sidebar + neon charts + card soup).
- Cheesy wedding kitsch (script fonts everywhere, pink glitter, hearts as UI chrome).
- Do not copy WeddingWire / The Knot / Zola / Joy / iCasei branding — they are functional references only (budget depth, checklist flow, all-in-one UX), never visual ones.

## Design Principles

1. **Números primeiro, sempre confiáveis** — financial figures are the product's core; they get hierarchy, tabular alignment and never ambiguity (every total is labeled: meta, previsto, fechado, pago, pendente, saldo).
2. **Uma olhada resolve** — each screen answers its main question in the first viewport on a phone: dashboard = "como estamos?", vendor = "o que falta pagar?", checklist = "o que fazer agora?".
3. **Calor sem kitsch** — warmth comes from palette, typography and spacing, not from wedding clichés.
4. **Celebrar progresso** — paid installments, completed tasks and the countdown are moments of delight, visibly acknowledged.
5. **Mobile é o palco principal** — every flow must be completable with one thumb; desktop is an enhancement.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG AA contrast minimums on all text and status colors.
- Status never communicated by color alone (always icon + label).
- Touch targets ≥ 44px; respects `prefers-reduced-motion`.
- pt-BR copy throughout; BRL currency; dd/MM/yyyy dates; America/Sao_Paulo.
